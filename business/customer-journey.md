# Customer Journey Map for Project-Boundary

## Phase 1: Aware
- **Trigger Event**: A project maintainer experiences scope creep in their open source project, leading to frustration and project delays.
- **Friction Points**: Lack of awareness about tools that can help manage project scope; overwhelming information about project management methodologies.
- **User Emotions**: Frustration, confusion, and a sense of being overwhelmed.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Educational content (blogs, webinars) on the importance of scope management; targeted ads showcasing the benefits of Project-Boundary.
- **Metric**: Number of website visits from targeted campaigns; engagement rate on educational content.

## Phase 2: Consider
- **Trigger Event**: The maintainer searches for solutions to manage project scope and comes across Project-Boundary.
- **Friction Points**: Uncertainty about the effectiveness of the tool; comparison with existing project management tools that may not focus on scope.
- **User Emotions**: Hopeful but cautious; seeking validation from peers or reviews.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: User testimonials, case studies, and a clear value proposition that highlights unique features of Project-Boundary.
- **Metric**: Number of demo requests or sign-ups for a trial; engagement with testimonials and case studies.

## Phase 3: Try
- **Trigger Event**: The maintainer decides to try Project-Boundary after being convinced by the value proposition.
- **Friction Points**: Initial setup complexity; learning curve associated with new tools.
- **User Emotions**: Excitement mixed with anxiety; eager to see results but worried about the time investment.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Onboarding support (tutorials, guides, customer support); easy integration with existing tools.
- **Metric**: Trial activation rate; time taken to complete the onboarding process.

## Phase 4: Adopt
- **Trigger Event**: The maintainer successfully uses Project-Boundary to define and manage project scope, seeing positive results.
- **Friction Points**: Potential resistance from team members who are accustomed to existing workflows; need for ongoing support.
- **User Emotions**: Satisfaction and relief; feeling empowered to manage projects more effectively.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Continuous engagement through updates, feature requests, and community forums; recognition of user achievements (e.g., badges).
- **Metric**: Retention rate post-trial; frequency of tool usage.

## Phase 5: Expand
- **Trigger Event**: The maintainer recognizes the value of Project-Boundary and considers using it for additional projects or recommending it to others.
- **Friction Points**: Budget constraints for open source projects; potential competition from other tools.
- **User Emotions**: Optimism about future projects; desire to share the success with the community.
- **Opportunities to Delight**: Referral incentives; showcasing success stories from other users; offering tiered pricing for larger teams or projects.
- **Metric**: Number of referrals; expansion into new projects or teams using Project-Boundary.
